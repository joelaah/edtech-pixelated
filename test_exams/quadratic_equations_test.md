## Q5
The sum of the roots of $3x^2 + 12x - 7 = 0$ is:

- [ ] $\frac{12}{3}$
- [x] $-4$
- [ ] $\frac{-7}{3}$
- [ ] $4$

**Points:** 2
**Type:** mcq

## Answer Key

### Q1
Factor the equation as $(x-2)(x-3) = 0$. Setting each factor to zero: $x - 2 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 2$ and $x - 3 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 3$.

### Q2
The discriminant formula is $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$. It determines the nature and number of roots of a quadratic equation. The other options are parts of the quadratic formula but not the discriminant itself.

### Q3
When $\Delta < 0$, the expression under the square root in the quadratic formula is negative, meaning there are no real solutions. The roots are complex conjugates: $x = \frac{-b \pm i\sqrt{|\Delta|}}{2a}$.

### Q4
The vertex form of a parabola is $y = a(x - h)^2 + k$, where $(h, k)$ is the vertex. Here $h = 3$ and $k = 5$, so the vertex is $(3, 5)$.

### Q5
By Vieta's formulas, the sum of roots $= \frac{-b}{a} = \frac{-12}{3} = -4$. The product of roots would be $\frac{c}{a} = \frac{-7}{3}$.
